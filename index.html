Who We Are

About GSAW

God Save Africa and the World (GSAW) is a political party founded in 2025 after 31 years of democracy in South Africa — based on the great number of unresolved challenges, merciless killing of women and children, looting of state resources, and the downgrading of the economy.

We see God as the only answer to all the challenges and problems of South Africa. GSAW is based on God's love, peace, justice, truth, holiness and forgiveness of sins.

This party believes that the whole world was created by God for His glory. The challenges the world faces — violence, crime, poverty, hunger, unemployment, abuse of power — God Almighty has all the power to deal perfectly with them. In this country, God should be our teacher, guider, director and leader.

Our Commitments

Aims & Objectives

From Article 2 of the GSAW Constitution — our foundational commitments to the people of South Africa.

01

Unity in Diversity

We believe in unity and diversity of all people. A united, non-racial, non-sexist and democratic country based on the principles of universal declaration of human rights and freedom.

02

Social Justice

To fight for social justice and eliminate inequalities. Fair and proper distribution of the land particularly for the poor, hungry and the disadvantaged.

03

Economic Development

To promote economic development for all people. We believe in a manufacturing, saving and investing world that empowers communities and creates jobs.

04

Anti-Corruption & Anti-Crime

To fight against all forms of corruption, crime and violence. Zero tolerance for abuse of power and state resources at all levels of government.

05

Total Human Liberation

To promote the freedom and liberation of a total man — political, social, spiritual, economic, financial, physical, and psychological.

06

Innovation & Creativity

We believe that people's minds should be given a space for creativity, innovation and invention. Trained professionals in their respective fields must guide us in solving our challenges.

Governance

Our Constitution

The GSAW Constitution governs the party's structure, membership, and operations. Key highlights below.

Membership (Art. 4)

  • Open to all people irrespective of colour, race and creed who accept its principles
  • Members must abide by the constitution
  • Members sign the Membership Application Form and pay the membership fee
  • Members should not belong to any other political party
  • Right to express positive views, elect and be elected

Structure (Art. 5)

  • National Convention → National Executive Committee (NEC)
  • Provincial Convention → Provincial Executive Committee (PEC)
  • Regional Convention → Regional Executive Committee
  • Branch AGM → Branch Executive Committee
  • Women's League & Youth League as autonomous bodies

National Convention (Art. 8-9)

  • Supreme policy-making body of GSAW
  • 95% delegates from active branch members
  • Determines policy, programme & constitution
  • Elects National Executive Committee
  • First convention scheduled for 2026, thereafter every 5 years

Discipline (Art. 20)

  • Actions against the Constitution will be disciplined
  • Actions against party policy or decisions
  • Any unlawful or criminal offence
  • Insults and fighting regarded as self-dismissal
  • Fair disciplinary process with right of appeal

Guiding Truths (Art. 3)

  • Believes in the Trinity — God the Father, Son, Holy Spirit
  • Only God can perfectly lead us to success and development
  • Believes in freedom of all humanity
  • Open to all who believe in the dominion of God Almighty
  • Prayer at each level is our priority

Branches (Art. 19)

  • Every member belongs to a branch (basic unit)
  • Minimum of 20 members per branch
  • Branches meet at least once per month
  • Branch executive meets at least once per fortnight
  • Registered with the Provincial Executive Committee
