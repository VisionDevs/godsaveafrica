Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about GSAW membership, branches, donations, and more.

Membership

Who can join GSAW?
Membership of GSAW is open to all people of the world irrespective of colour, race and creed who accept its principles, policy, programmes and all rules. You must be 18 years or older and hold a valid SA ID number.
How much is the membership fee?
Membership fees are determined by your branch executive. After your application is approved, your branch leader will contact you via WhatsApp with payment details. Fees are typically affordable and help fund local branch activities.
How long does approval take?
Applications are typically reviewed within 24-48 hours. You will receive a WhatsApp message from your branch leader with your membership number and a link to join the members group. You can check your status here.
Can I belong to another political party?
No. As per the GSAW Constitution (Article 4), members shall not belong to any other political party. Dual membership is not permitted. If you are currently a member of another party, you must resign before joining GSAW.
How do I check my application status?
Visit our Status Checker page and enter your 13-digit SA ID number or your membership number (e.g., GSAW-25-AB3K7). You'll see your current status immediately.

Branches & Structure

What is a branch?
A branch is the basic unit of GSAW. Every member belongs to a branch in their area. A branch requires a minimum of 20 members and must be registered with the Provincial Executive Committee. Branches meet at least once per month.
How do I find my branch?
When your membership is approved, you'll be assigned to a branch based on your ward and municipality. Your branch leader will contact you via WhatsApp and add you to the branch group. If there's no branch in your area yet, you may be asked to help establish one!
Can I start a branch in my area?
Yes! If there are at least 20 members in your ward/area, you can apply to form a branch. Contact your Provincial Executive Committee through the party's WhatsApp group or contact us for guidance.

Donations

How can I donate to GSAW?
Visit our Donations page to make a contribution. You can choose a preset amount or enter a custom amount. Upload your proof of payment (EFT receipt) and our treasurer will verify it.
Do I need to be a member to donate?
No. Anyone — individuals and organisations — can donate to GSAW. All donations are used for party operations, community outreach, and election campaigns. We are transparent about how funds are used.

Elections & Voting

Is GSAW registered with the IEC?
GSAW is in the process of registration with the Independent Electoral Commission (IEC). Once registered, we will contest local government and national elections. Join now to be part of building the party from the ground up.
Can I stand as a candidate?
Yes! All members in good standing have the right to be elected or appointed to any committee, and to stand as a candidate for ward, municipal, provincial, or national elections. Candidates are nominated through branch and provincial structures.

Discipline & Rules

What can get me expelled?
Grounds for discipline include: any action against the Constitution, policy, decisions, rules or regulations of GSAW; unlawful or criminal offences; insults and fighting (regarded as self-dismissal); and joining another political party while being a GSAW member.
