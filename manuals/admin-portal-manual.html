Admin Portal Overview

What the portal does and who uses it

The GSAW Admin Portal is the internal management system for the National Executive Committee. It allows authorised staff to manage all data submitted through the public website — including membership applications, donations, volunteers, and contact messages.

🌐 Portal URL

www.gsaw.org.za/admin
Access from any browser — desktop or mobile.

🔐 Access Control

Login is required. Only authorised NEC staff with the admin credentials may access this portal.

💾 Database

All data is stored securely in Supabase (cloud database). No data is lost when you close the browser.

📱 Mobile Friendly

The portal works on smartphones and tablets. Use the ☰ menu icon on mobile to navigate.

Logging In & Logging Out

How to access the admin portal securely

PasswordGsaw@Admin@2025
Keep these credentials strictly confidential. Do not write them on paper or share via WhatsApp or social media.

How to Log In

  1. Open your browser (Chrome recommended) and go to www.gsaw.org.za/admin
  2. The login screen will appear. Enter the email: admin@gsaw.org.za
  3. Enter the password: Gsaw@Admin@2025
  4. Click the Login button
  5. You will be taken directly to the Dashboard
Your session is saved in the browser. If you close and reopen the tab, you may not need to log in again on the same device.

How to Log Out

  1. Look at the bottom of the left sidebar
  2. Click the 🚪 Logout button
  3. You will be returned to the login screen
Always log out when using a shared or public computer.

Dashboard

Your overview of all GSAW activity at a glance

The Dashboard is the first screen you see after logging in. It gives you a real-time summary of all activity on the website.

Stat Cards (Top Row)

CardWhat it Shows
📋 Total MembersTotal number of membership applications received (all statuses)
⏳ PendingApplications that have not yet been approved — needs action
✅ ApprovedMembers who have been approved and are official GSAW members
🗺️ ProvincesNumber of provinces represented by current members

Membership Growth Chart

A line chart showing new membership applications per week. This helps you track if the party is growing. The x-axis shows weeks, the y-axis shows the number of new applications that week.

Volunteer Growth Chart

A bar chart showing new volunteer sign-ups per week (orange bars). This lets you see volunteer engagement trends over time.

Recent Applications

A list of the 5 most recent membership applications so you can quickly see who just joined and take action without needing to navigate to the Applications view.

Refreshing Data

  1. Click the 🔄 Refresh button in the top toolbar
  2. The button will spin and say "Refreshing…" while it fetches the latest data
  3. All stats, charts, and tables update automatically once done
Data refreshes automatically each time you switch between views in the sidebar. You only need to click Refresh to see changes made by another admin in real time.

Membership Applications

Review, approve, and manage all membership submissions

This view shows all membership applications — both pending and approved. New applications submitted from the public website appear here first with a pending status.

Navigating to Applications

  1. Click Applications in the left sidebar (clipboard icon)
  2. The full list of applications loads with the most recent at the top

Searching & Filtering

ToolHow to Use
Search BoxType a name, email, phone number, or ID number to instantly filter results
Province FilterClick the Province dropdown to show only members from a specific province
Status FilterChoose All / Pending / Approved to filter by application status

Viewing Application Details

  1. Find the application in the table
  2. Click the 👁 View button (eye icon) in the Actions column
  3. A detail panel opens showing all information: personal details, address, ID number, skills, reason for joining, signature, and timestamps
  4. From the detail panel you can also approve or delete the application

Approving an Application

  1. Find a pending application in the table
  2. Click the ✓ Approve button in the Actions column
  3. A confirmation popup will appear — click Confirm
  4. The application status changes to approved immediately
  5. The member now appears in the Approved Members view
After approving, go to the Approved Members view and send the member a WhatsApp welcome message with their membership number.

Deleting an Application

  1. Click the 🗑 Delete button (red trash icon) next to the application
  2. A confirmation popup appears — click Delete to confirm
Deletion is permanent. Only delete duplicate, test, or fraudulent applications.

Bulk Actions (Multiple Deletions)

  1. Click the ⚡ Bulk Actions button in the toolbar
  2. Checkboxes appear on each row — tick all the applications you want to act on
  3. Click Delete Selected to remove them all at once
  4. Click Bulk Actions again to hide the checkboxes

Exporting to CSV / Excel

  1. Click the ⬇ Export CSV button in the toolbar
  2. A CSV file downloads to your device
  3. Open the file in Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets
Export regularly and save copies as your official membership register backup.

Pagination (Large Lists)

If there are many applications, they are split into pages. Use the < > arrow buttons at the bottom of the table to navigate between pages. The current page and total count are shown in the middle (e.g., "Page 2 of 5 — 48 total").

This view shows only approved members. It is designed for the NEC to manage and communicate with official members after approval.

SkillsSkills they listed on the application
ActionsView details, send WhatsApp welcome message

Sending a WhatsApp Welcome Message

After a member is approved, you should send them a welcome WhatsApp message with their official membership number and the GSAW Members Group link.

  1. Find the approved member in the Approved Members list
  2. Click the 💬 Message button (WhatsApp icon)
  3. WhatsApp will open on your device — the message is pre-filled with:
      • A personalised greeting with the member's first name
      • Their GSAW Membership Number
      • A link to join the official GSAW Members WhatsApp Group
  4. Review the message and press Send in WhatsApp
This is the primary way to welcome new members and get them into the Members Group. Always do this within 24 hours of approval.
The WhatsApp message button is only in the Approved view — not in the general Applications view — to ensure you only contact confirmed members.

Members by Province

Geographic distribution of GSAW membership

This view shows how membership is distributed across South Africa's 9 provinces. It is useful for understanding where GSAW's support base is strongest and where outreach is needed.

Province Bar Chart

A horizontal bar chart shows all 9 provinces side by side. There are two data sets shown:

  • Green bars — Total applications (pending + approved) from that province
  • Orange bars — Approved members only from that province

Hover over any bar to see the exact count. The chart is sorted alphabetically.

Province Breakdown List

Below the chart, each province has a row showing:

  • The province name
  • A green progress bar showing its share of total membership
  • Total count and Approved count side by side

Provinces are sorted from most members to fewest.

Donations

Track and verify all donations made to GSAW

This view shows all donation submissions from the public website. All donations are currently processed via Bank Transfer (EFT) — online payment gateways are being configured and will be added soon.

Donation Record Columns

ColumnDescription
DonorName of the individual or organisation who donated
AmountDonation amount in Rands (R)
PurposeWhat the donation is for (e.g., General Fund, Campaign, Events)
Statuspending = not yet confirmed   verified = payment confirmed
Proof of PaymentIcon shows if a POP was uploaded. Click to view the document.
DateWhen the donation record was submitted

Verifying a Donation (After Bank Confirmation)

  1. Log in to your bank and confirm the EFT payment was received
  2. Find the donation in the Donations view — it will show as pending
  3. Click 👁 View to see full donation details
  4. If the Proof of Payment was uploaded, click the POP icon to view and verify it matches
  5. Click the ✓ Verify button to mark the donation as verified
Check donations daily. Contact the donor via the phone/email shown if proof of payment is missing.

GSAW Bank Details (for Reference)

Bank details are shown directly on the public donate page at www.gsaw.org.za/donate. Donors are instructed to use their name as the payment reference.

Volunteers

Manage all GSAW volunteer registrations

This view lists everyone who has submitted a volunteer application via the public website. Volunteers are separate from members — they are people who want to actively assist GSAW but may not yet be official members.

Volunteer Record Columns

ColumnDescription

Areas of HelpWhat they offered to help with (e.g., Campaigns, Media, Admin, Community Outreach)
AvailabilityWhen they are available (Weekdays / Weekends / Evenings)
DateWhen they registered

Searching Volunteers

Use the Search box at the top to filter by name, province, email, or area. This is useful when looking for volunteers in a specific area for an upcoming event or campaign.

Contacting a Volunteer

  1. Find the volunteer in the list
  2. Click their phone number to open WhatsApp or call
  3. Click their email to open your email client with their address pre-filled
When coordinating provincial campaigns, filter volunteers by province first to quickly find local helpers.

Contact Messages

Read and respond to all public enquiries

All messages submitted via the Contact Us page on the public website appear here. The public sender automatically receives an email confirmation when they submit.

Reading Messages

  1. Click Messages in the left sidebar
  2. Unread messages are highlighted — click any message to expand and read the full content
  3. After reading, click Mark as Read to clear the unread highlight

Replying to a Message

  1. Open the message you want to reply to
  2. Click the Reply via Email button
  3. Your email client opens with a pre-drafted reply template already filled in, including the sender's email, subject, and a professional GSAW response template
  4. Personalise the reply body where indicated and click Send
The reply template includes the GSAW signature with the website, phone number, and email. Do not change the signature.
The reply will be sent from info@gsaw.org.za — make sure you are logged in to that email account on your device before clicking Reply.

Tips & Best Practices

Keeping the portal running smoothly

Daily Routine

  • Check Pending Applications and approve or follow up within 24 hours
  • Check Donations against your bank statement and verify received payments
  • Check Messages and reply within 1 business day

Data Security

  • Never share the admin password via WhatsApp, SMS, or social media
  • Log out of the portal whenever using a shared device
  • Export and save a CSV backup of members at least once per week
  • If you suspect unauthorised access, change the password immediately via Supabase dashboard

If Something Looks Wrong

  • If the dashboard shows no data — click the 🔄 Refresh button
  • If login fails — check that you are using the correct email and password
  • If a chart is blank — there may be no data yet; it will populate as real submissions come in
  • For technical issues, contact the system developer
