Membership Application (for Public)

What the public sees and how the process works end-to-end

When a member of the public wants to join GSAW, they go to www.gsaw.org.za/membership and fill in the application form. Understanding this process helps you explain it to potential members.

What the Applicant Fills In

  • Personal details: Full name, ID number, date of birth, gender
  • Contact details: Email address, phone number
  • Address: Street, city, province, postal code
  • Political details: Municipality, ward number, voting station name
  • Background: Occupation, qualifications, skills, reason for joining
  • Digital signature: Drawn on screen to accept the POPIA consent

What Happens After Submission

  1. The application is saved to the GSAW database with a pending status
  2. A unique Membership Reference Number is generated (e.g., GSAW-2026-00001)
  3. The applicant receives an automatic confirmation email from info@gsaw.org.za
  4. The NEC admin receives a notification email at admin@gsaw.org.za
  5. The NEC reviews the application in the Admin Portal and either approves or rejects it
  6. Once approved, the applicant can check their status at gsaw.org.za/status
  7. The NEC sends a WhatsApp welcome message with their membership number and the Members Group link
Direct potential members to www.gsaw.org.za/membership or share the link via WhatsApp. The form works on mobile.

Application Status Check

How members track their application progress

Once a member has submitted their application, they can check its status at any time — without logging in — by going to www.gsaw.org.za/status.

How to Check Status

  1. Go to www.gsaw.org.za/status
  2. Enter either your ID Number or your Email Address that you used when applying
  3. Click Check Status
  4. Your application details and current status will be displayed:
      • ⏳ Pending — Application received, under review
      • ✅ Approved — You are an official GSAW member
      • Not Found — ID/email not in the system
Share this page with applicants after they submit. It prevents them calling in to ask about their status.

Donations — EFT Process

How the donation form works and what donors need to do

Donations are made at www.gsaw.org.za/donate. Currently only EFT (bank transfer) is accepted. Online payment gateways (Yoco, SnapScan, Ozow) will be available in a future update.

Donor Process — Step by Step

  1. Donor goes to www.gsaw.org.za/donate
  2. Chooses donor type: Individual or Organisation
  3. Fills in their name, contact details, donation amount and purpose
  4. Selects Bank Transfer (EFT) as the payment method
  5. The GSAW bank details are displayed on screen
  6. Donor does the EFT from their bank and uploads their Proof of Payment (PDF or image)
  7. Clicks Submit Donation
  8. A confirmation email is sent to the donor automatically
  9. The NEC admin verifies the donation in the Admin Portal once payment reflects

Bank Details (Shown on Donate Page)

The GSAW bank account details are shown directly on the donation page. The reference for payment should be the donor's full name. Contact the NEC treasurer for the official bank account number.

Volunteer Registration

How people sign up to help GSAW

Anyone who wants to volunteer for GSAW (whether or not they are a member) can register at www.gsaw.org.za/volunteer.

What Volunteers Fill In

  • First name and last name
  • Email address and phone number
  • Province they are in
  • Areas they can help with (multiple selection):
    Campaigns, Community Outreach, Media & Communications, Admin & Office Support, Events, Youth Programmes, Legal, Finance
  • Availability: Weekdays / Weekends / Evenings / Flexible
  • Optional message — anything they want the NEC to know

After Submission

  1. The volunteer record is saved to the database
  2. The volunteer receives a confirmation email from info@gsaw.org.za
  3. The NEC admin receives a notification email at admin@gsaw.org.za
  4. The volunteer is visible in the Admin Portal under Volunteers
  5. The NEC contacts the volunteer via phone/WhatsApp to assign them tasks
Encourage all members and supporters to also register as volunteers — even if they can only help on weekends or online.

Contact Us Form

How the public reaches GSAW through the website

Members of the public can send a message to GSAW at www.gsaw.org.za/contact.

Contact Form Fields

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Subject
  • Message

What Happens When Someone Contacts GSAW

  1. The message is saved to the database
  2. The sender receives an auto-reply email confirming their message was received
  3. The NEC admin sees the message in the Admin Portal under Messages
  4. Admin clicks Reply via Email in the portal — a pre-drafted professional reply opens in the email client
  5. Admin personalises and sends the reply from info@gsaw.org.za
Aim to reply to all contact messages within 1 business day. Unanswered messages reflect poorly on the party.

Setting Up Email on iPhone (iOS)

How to add a GSAW email account to the built-in Mail app

You Will Need (Get From Your Email Host)

Email Addressinfo@gsaw.org.za or admin@gsaw.org.za
PasswordYour mailbox password (from your email hosting provider)
Incoming Mail Servere.g., mail.gsaw.org.za — provided by host
IMAP Port993 (SSL) or 143
Outgoing (SMTP) Servere.g., smtp.gsaw.org.za — provided by host
SMTP Port465 (SSL) or 587 (TLS)

Steps to Add the Account

  1. Open Settings on your iPhone
  2. Scroll down and tap Mail
  3. Tap Accounts
  4. Tap Add Account
  5. Tap Other (not Gmail or Microsoft)
  6. Tap Add Mail Account
  7. Fill in:
      • Name: GSAW (or your name)
      • Email: info@gsaw.org.za
      • Password: your mailbox password
      • Description: GSAW Info Email
  8. Tap Next
  9. Select IMAP at the top
  10. Enter the Incoming Mail Server details (from your host)
  11. Enter the Outgoing Mail Server (SMTP) details
  12. Tap Next → tap Save
  13. Your GSAW email will now appear in the Mail app
Repeat the same process for the second email account (admin@gsaw.org.za). Both accounts will appear in your Mail app side by side.

Setting Up Email on Android

How to add a GSAW email account using Gmail or the Samsung Email app

Option A — Using the Gmail App (Recommended)

  1. Open the Gmail app on your Android phone
  2. Tap your profile picture (top right) → tap Add another account
  3. Scroll down and select Other
  4. Enter your GSAW email: info@gsaw.org.za → tap Next
  5. Select Personal (IMAP)
  6. Enter your mailbox password → tap Next
  7. Enter the Incoming server settings:
      • Username: info@gsaw.org.za
      • Password: your password
      • IMAP server: provided by your host
      • Port: 993   Security type: SSL/TLS
  8. Tap Next → enter Outgoing (SMTP) server settings
  9. Tap Next → configure sync options → tap Next
  10. Give the account a name (e.g., "GSAW Info") and tap Done

Option B — Using Samsung Email App

  1. Open Samsung Email app
  2. Tap the menu (☰) → tap Settings → tap Add account
  3. Select Others
  4. Enter your email address and password → tap Manual Setup
  5. Choose IMAP
  6. Fill in the incoming and outgoing server details from your email host
  7. Tap Done
The Gmail app works on all Android phones (Samsung, Huawei, etc.) and is easier to set up than manufacturer email apps. It is the recommended option.

Setting Up Email on Windows PC (Outlook)

How to add a GSAW email to Microsoft Outlook on a desktop or laptop

Using Microsoft Outlook (Office 365 / Outlook 2019/2021)

  1. Open Microsoft Outlook
  2. Click File in the top menu → click Add Account
  3. Enter your GSAW email: info@gsaw.org.za → click Connect
  4. If Outlook cannot auto-detect settings, select IMAP manually
  5. Fill in the Incoming mail settings:
      • Server: your IMAP server (from host)
      • Port: 993   Encryption: SSL/TLS
  6. Fill in the Outgoing mail settings:
      • Server: your SMTP server (from host)
      • Port: 587   Encryption: STARTTLS
  7. Click Next → enter your password → click Connect
  8. Click Done — your GSAW email will now appear in Outlook's left panel

Using Windows Mail (Built-in App)

  1. Open the Mail app (Windows 10/11)
  2. Click the ⚙️ Settings cog → click Manage Accounts
  3. Click Add account
  4. Select Other account (POP, IMAP)
  5. Enter your GSAW email, display name, and password
  6. Click Sign in — if it fails, click Advanced setup and enter IMAP/SMTP details manually
If Outlook asks to verify your identity or shows a security warning about the server certificate, contact your email hosting provider for the correct SSL settings.

Testing the Account

  1. Once set up, send a test email from info@gsaw.org.za to your personal email
  2. Check that the email arrives and the sender shows as info@gsaw.org.za
  3. Reply from your personal email to info@gsaw.org.za and confirm it arrives in the GSAW inbox
