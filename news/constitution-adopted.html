GSAW Constitution Formally Adopted

The God Save Africa and the World (GSAW) Constitution has been formally adopted by the founding members, establishing the governance framework that will guide our party from its founding through to its first National Convention and beyond.

A Framework for Godly Governance

The Constitution establishes GSAW as a Christ-centered political party committed to quality service delivery, eradication of corruption, and the total transformation of South Africa through Biblical principles. It defines our organisational structure from National to Branch level, membership requirements, leadership roles, and disciplinary procedures.

"GSAW has been founded in 2025 after 31 years of democracy in South Africa based on the great number of challenges and unresolved problems and massive, merciless killing of women and children, looting of state resources, and downgrading of the economy. We see God as the only answer to all the challenges and problems of South Africa." — GSAW Constitution, Preamble

Key Constitutional Provisions

The Constitution covers 23 Articles addressing:

  • Article 2: Aims and Objectives — Unity, social justice, economic development, anti-corruption
  • Article 3: Guiding Truths — Trinity, God's leadership, freedom for all humanity
  • Article 4: Membership — Open to all irrespective of colour, race and creed
  • Articles 5-7: Organisational Structure from National to Branch level
  • Articles 8-10: National Convention and NEC powers
  • Article 13: Duties of officials (President, Deputy, Chairperson, Secretary General, Treasurer)
  • Articles 14-19: Provincial, Regional and Branch structures
  • Article 20: Discipline

What This Means for Members

The adopted Constitution provides clear rules for membership, elections, and accountability. All members are now bound by these provisions. The first National Convention is scheduled for 2026, at which point the National Executive Committee will be formally elected.

The full Constitution is available for download on our Constitution page.
